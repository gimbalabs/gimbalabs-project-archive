# Funding and Proposal writing guidance (WIP)

## 🏴‍☠️ Draft: “Gimbalabs Funding Code” (Working Title)

*this is WIP - Work in progress.  Iterations may vary greatly*

### 1. Purpose

To provide fair, transparent, and principled guidance for how Gimbalabs allocates community time, attention, and treasury funds — while staying true to our driver:

> To provide open, replicable, safe spaces to learn, explore, and empower individuals and organizations anywhere so that we can solve meaningful problems.
> 

This isn’t a legal policy. It’s a **shared understanding** — a compass, not a cage.

---

### 2. Core Principles (“Articles of the Code”)

**1. Open Contribution over Open Source**

Open source is encouraged, but not required. What matters is **open contribution** — so that Gimbalabs members have opportunities to participate, learn from, and build upon funded work.

**2. Alignment before Funding**

We fund projects that align with the Gimbalabs driver — advancing education, collaboration, or tools that empower the ecosystem. If it benefits both the proposer and Gimbalabs, it’s likely a good fit.

**3. Context Matters**

Funding decisions are made case-by-case. Some closed-source projects may be justified (e.g., for security or business reasons) if they still serve the broader mission.

**4. Mutual Benefit, Not Extraction**

When Gimbalabs supports a for-profit venture, the community should receive some form of reciprocal value — a stake, shared ownership, learning outcomes, or reinvestment into the commons.

**5. Transparency of Intention**

Proposals must clearly state whether they are open or closed source, for-profit or non-profit, and how the community benefits. Hidden motives erode trust.

**6. Sustainability is a Shared Responsibility**

Profit motive is not a bug — it’s a feature when used responsibly. Gimbalabs should sustain itself through cycles of value creation and reinvestment.

**7. Consent Over Consensus**

Funding decisions are made through the Sociocracy 3.0 consent process. Objections are welcomed as opportunities for improvement, not resistance.

**8. Learning Is the Ultimate Output**

Current projects are reviewed monthly at Gimbalabs Open Spaces, on the last Thursday of every month. Teams from funded projects come prepared to share what they have learned and share artifacts from their work. Acceptable artifacts include code demos, documentation, video links, blog posts, or brief presentations.

---

### 3. How to Propose Funding (Short Form)

- **Step 1:** Draft your proposal using the [Gimbalabs proposal template](https://www.notion.so/1290db6d1d5d80ff9d38de31cddbcfc2?pvs=21)
- **Step 2:** Share the idea in Discord or an Open Space session.
- **Step 3:** Incorporate feedback and align with the Gimbalabs driver.
- **Step 4:** Present for consent during a Thursday Open Space community meeting.
- **Step 5:** Once consented, coordinate with admins for Notion access, calendar listings, and milestone tracking.
