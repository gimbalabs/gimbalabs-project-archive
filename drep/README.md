# Gimbalabs DRep

Gimbalabs operates as a collective DRep (Delegated Representative) in Cardano's on-chain governance system.

## Mission

To represent the Gimbalabs community in Cardano governance decisions, guided by our primary driver:

> To provide open, replicable safe spaces to learn, to explore, and to empower individuals and organizations anywhere so that we can solve meaningful problems

## Delegation

To delegate your voting power to Gimbalabs DRep:

**DRep ID:** `[drep_id_here]`

You can delegate via:
- [Gov.Tools](https://gov.tools)
- [Eternl Wallet](https://eternl.io)
- [Lace Wallet](https://www.lace.io)
- Any governance-enabled Cardano wallet

## Our Approach

Gimbalabs DRep voting decisions are made collectively using a consent-based process:

1. **Identification** - Monitor gov.tools and community channels for governance actions
2. **Analysis** - Prepare unbiased summaries of proposals
3. **Discussion** - Open discussion in Discord and Open Spaces
4. **Decision** - Consent-based decision in community meetings
5. **Documentation** - Record vote and rationale
6. **Publication** - Submit vote on-chain with published reasoning

## Values

When evaluating governance actions, we consider:

- **Experimentation over perfection** - Is this "good enough for now, safe enough to try"?
- **Education and empowerment** - Does this help people learn and participate?
- **Technical resilience** - Does this strengthen Cardano's infrastructure?
- **Decentralization** - Does this distribute power appropriately?
- **Transparency** - Are the proposers clear about intentions and conflicts?

## Conflicts of Interest

We disclose relevant relationships:
- Gimbalabs is a member of Intersect
- Individual community members may have relationships with proposing organizations

These are noted in vote rationales when relevant.

## Vote Records

All votes are recorded in `/drep/votes/` with full rationales.

See [process.md](./process.md) for our detailed decision-making process.

## Participation

Community members can participate by:
- Joining discussions in Discord `#governance` channel
- Attending Open Spaces governance segments
- Raising objections during consent periods
- Proposing how we should vote on specific actions
