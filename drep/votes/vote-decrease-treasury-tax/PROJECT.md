# Vote on "Decrease Treasury Tax from 20% to 10%"

## Gimbalabs Primary Driver

To provide open, replicable safe spaces to learn, to explore, and to empower individuals and organizations anywhere so that we can solve meaningful problems

## Proposal Driver

Gimbalabs is a governance DRep within the Cardano Ecosystem and is required to maintain liveliness of voting. As long as active and interested Gimbalabs' members reach a consensus on the direction of such governance actions Gimbalabs should vote in a DRep capacity. In any worse-case scenario the Gimbalab DRep account may abstain from voting. In all cases rationale should be provided, being wholly transparent to the broader Cardano community, in supporting the voting direction. Also consider including any conflicts of interest within the rationale.

By participating in Cardano governance Gimbalabs is showing its interest and, at times, concern about the direction and longevity of the Cardano blockchain. Publicly, this can be impactful to Gimbalabs reputation, its own membership and provides insight into where Gimbalabs convictions lie. By being open and public with vote rationale and various positions Gimbalabs can be considered a model organization that others replicate. It will show that it is a community of opinions with a voice that are listened to, acknowledged and may impact Cardano's future.

## Proposal

### 1. Brief Description

This proposal is to justify a YES vote on the Parameter Change below.

> The proposal seeks to reduce the treasury cut from 20% to 10%, increasing staking rewards and incentivizing greater participation in governance. This adjustment strengthens decentralization and network security while maintaining a sustainable treasury.

### 4. Details of the Proposal

This proposal should be justified by Cardano's staking participation rates, treasury utilization, and historical transaction volume. Rather than relying on direct comparisons to external blockchains, the focus should be on positioning Cardano as a highly competitive ecosystem that maximizes staking incentives and treasury efficiency. Ensuring an optimal balance between treasury funding and staking rewards will attract users and developers, particularly during the most competitive phases of market cycles, reinforcing Cardano's long-term adoption and growth.

(source: https://gov.tools/governance_actions/941502b0aa104c850d197923259444d2b57cab7af18b63143775465aaacc84f5#0)

### 5. Impact and Benefits

The pros and cons listed below have been modified from the on-chain rationale. These are not intended to be complete lists.

**PROS:**
- Potentially forces funded proposers to build products that increase chain usage since there will be reduced funds availability; the chain will require more transactions to collect more fees
- This is an appropriate experiment of governance and the parameter can be reversed or modified in time if side-effects prove too drastic
- More tx fees go to staked wallet holders
- It can further decentralize (only the security of) the blockchain; but how much is enough, or too much
- It's certainly not going to destroy the blockchain
- Either way the whales and founders still win but maybe some of the community can receive compensation for the knowledge sharing and extractive nature of all things Cardano
- Possibly increase funding responsibility, efficiency and distribution

**CONS:**
- I believe the onboarding opportunity for new ADA holders would still remain low and it will have minimal effects on tx count or governance participation, thus negating the above
- CEX and other key holders will also increase their returns
- This further increases the size of the wallets at the top
- Lower funding availability for proposers
