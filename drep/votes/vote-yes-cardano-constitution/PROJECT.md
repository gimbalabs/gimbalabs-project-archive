# Vote Yes on Cardano Constitution to Replace the Interim Constitution

## Gimbalabs Primary Driver

To provide open, replicable safe spaces to learn, to explore, and to empower individuals and organizations anywhere so that we can solve meaningful problems

## Proposal Driver

Gimbalabs is running experiments in shared governance among a loose collective of stakeholders with different levels of commitment to our shared work. One of the experiments we're running is to learn whether the consent process we've been practicing is a way to make decisions about how our Gimbalabs DRep will vote on Cardano governance actions. The first decision to make is a big one: how should we vote on the proposal to replace Cardano's interim constitution with the one that was drafted and ratified by the Cardano community in 2024?

The Gimbalabs DRep should vote "Yes" on this proposal. By doing so, we will state that the new constitution is "good enough for now, and safe enough to try". A yes vote would be aligned with the pragmatic approach that has defined Gimbalabs since its founding in 2020: embracing progress over perfection so that we can learn as much as possible and then improve. When we vote to drive experimentation forward, even while recognizing that the constitution is imperfect, we are more likely to create spaces to learn, to explore, and to empower individuals to keep defining and solving problems.

## Criticisms Against the Constitution (Why Vote NO?)

1. **Vague & Incomplete** - Key terms (e.g., "community") are undefined, leaving room for manipulation.
2. **Too Hard to Amend** - The high threshold for changes could lead to power stagnation.
3. **Limited Community Influence** - Without backing from Cardano's founders & Intersect MBO, ordinary users have little real control.
4. **Low Participation in Drafting** - Despite millions spent, actual engagement was weak.
5. **Intersect Holds Too Much Power** - Governance feels controlled rather than decentralized.
6. **Technical Issues** - The governance platform has inefficiencies, leading to wasted resources.
7. **No Strong Decentralization Guarantees** - There are no clear mechanisms ensuring fair power distribution.

## Defenses for the Constitution (Why Vote YES?)

1. **It's a Start, Not the Final Version** - The Constitution is a living document that can evolve.
2. **High Amendment Threshold Ensures Stability** - Prevents bad actors from making reckless changes.
3. **Community Was Involved in the Process** - 63 workshops, 51 countries, and 95% approval from elected delegates.
4. **Low Participation Was a Choice** - The process was open, but not everyone engaged.
5. **Intersect Facilitates, It Doesn't Dictate** - Its role is to manage governance, not own it.
6. **Technical Issues Are Fixable** - Governance tools will improve over time.
7. **Decentralization Takes Time** - Governance needs to grow alongside the ecosystem.
