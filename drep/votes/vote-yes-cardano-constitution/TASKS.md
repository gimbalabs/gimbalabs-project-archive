## Notes

This project documented Gimbalabs DRep's decision to vote Yes on the Cardano Constitution to Replace the Interim Constitution.

**Governance Action:**
- URL: https://gov.tools/governance_actions/8c653ee5c9800e6d31e79b5a7f7d4400c81d44717ad4db633dc18d4c07e4a4fd#0
- Gov Action ID: `gov_action133jnaewfsq8x6v08ndd87l2yqryp63r30t2dkceacxx5cply5n7sqzlcyqf`
- Vote submitted: 2025-02-13

**Official Rationale:**
The new constitution is "good enough for now, and safe enough to try". Our yes vote is aligned with the pragmatic approach that has defined Gimbalabs since its founding in 2020: embracing progress over perfection so that we can learn as much as possible and then improve.

**Acknowledged Concerns:**
- High amendment threshold
- The need to be informed and engaged about Governance
- Conflict of interest as member of Intersect

**Criticisms Considered:**
1. Vague & Incomplete - Key terms undefined
2. Too Hard to Amend - High threshold could lead to stagnation
3. Limited Community Influence
4. Low Participation in Drafting
5. Intersect Holds Too Much Power
6. Technical Issues
7. No Strong Decentralization Guarantees

**Defenses Considered:**
1. It's a Start, Not the Final Version
2. High Amendment Threshold Ensures Stability
3. Community Was Involved (63 workshops, 51 countries, 95% approval)
4. Low Participation Was a Choice
5. Intersect Facilitates, It Doesn't Dictate
6. Technical Issues Are Fixable
7. Decentralization Takes Time
