# Vote Yes on Cardano Blockchain Ecosystem Budget: Amaru Node Development 2025

## Gimbalabs Primary Driver

To provide open, replicable safe spaces to learn, to explore, and to empower individuals and organizations anywhere so that we can solve meaningful problems

## Proposal Driver

The Cardano blockchain currently relies on a single production node implementation (in Haskell), creating a potential single point of failure and limiting the ecosystem's technical diversity and resilience. A team of developers is building Amaru, a Rust implementation of the Cardano node, and is seeking funding through the Cardano Blockchain Ecosystem Budget.

As an organization dedicated to empowering individuals and solving meaningful problems in the Cardano ecosystem, Gimbalabs' ability to provide safe learning spaces is impacted by the ecosystem's technical foundation. **A single node implementation limits diversity of development approaches, creates systemic risk, and narrows the pathways for developers to contribute.**

Gimbalabs needs to actively support initiatives that strengthen Cardano's technical foundation and create more pathways for developer participation, directly aligning with our mission to provide open, replicable safe spaces to learn and explore. Additionally, as a Delegated Representative (DREP) in Cardano's governance system, Gimbalabs has a responsibility to celebrate on-chain voting processes that demonstrate the effectiveness of direct governance mechanisms.

By supporting the Amaru Node Development through our DREP voting power, we'll help diversify Cardano's technical implementation, reduce systemic risk, improve specifications that enable further implementations, and demonstrate our commitment to direct on-chain governance using Cardano's native tooling. This action reinforces our belief that active participation in governance is essential for a healthy, decentralized ecosystem and sets an example for other organizations in the space.

### 5. Impact and Benefits

- A quick Yes vote on this Info Action will signal that Gimbalabs wants to see this kind of direct budgeting, using the native tooling of Cardano
- The Amaru proposal is thoughtful and complete. It sets a strong precedent by establishing a high standard for future proposals to live up to.
- Bringing a second Cardano Node to production will make Cardano more resilient and future-ready.
- The Amaru team is not focused only on building a Rust node, but also on improving the specifications in a way that will make it easier to work on additional implementations: "Developing a new implementation of existing software means dissecting every part of the original implementation. Along the way, bugs and discrepancies with the specification are commonly found through conformance testing. Gaps in the documentation can also be identified and addressed. The development of Amaru has already proven this on several occasions. In the long run, more node implementations can be a synonym for reduced development time by allowing all implementations to converge faster towards better designs and more robust implementations."
- Improved DevX and Learning Interface: "Observability is challenging, and it is even more so when rolling your own solution. With Amaru, we intend to embrace industry standards for monitoring, tracing, and logging to drastically enhance the user experience for operators seeking insights into their node's behaviour."
