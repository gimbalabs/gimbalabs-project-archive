#### Gap Analysis

**SLT 1.1: "I can categorize Gimbalabs' completed and active projects by theme and structure."**
- **Partial dimensions**: Assessment — I can evaluate whether a categorization is internally consistent, but community members may see categories that matter to them that don't appear in the written docs (e.g., "projects that built trust" or "projects that burned people out").

- **Risk if coached without context**: Lesson surfaces obvious patterns (PBL courses repeat, Catalyst is the main funder) but misses the uncomfortable or non-obvious ones.

#### Gap Analysis

**SLT 4.1: "I can map Gimbalabs' funding history and its concentration risks."**
- **Partial dimensions**: Conceptual (budget numbers are in project files but full treasury picture — GimbalPool rewards, reserves, debt history — is not all in the repo), Currency (funding situation changes).
- **What I would need**: Treasury overview — total inflows by source, current reserves, burn rate. The 2025 sustainability project mentions debt resolution; what's the current financial picture?
- **Risk if coached without context**: Lesson presents incomplete funding data as the whole picture.

**SLT 4.2: "I can propose alternative sustainability models."**
- **Partial dimensions**: Assessment — I know sustainability models broadly (fee-for-service, grants, sponsorship, consulting, token incentives, earned revenue) but evaluating whether a specific model is realistic for Gimbalabs requires understanding the community's capacity, appetite for commercial work, and constraints.
- **What I would need**: Constraints — what has the community tried and rejected? What's off the table? What's the appetite for revenue-generating work vs. pure public goods?
- **Risk if coached without context**: Lesson proposes models the community has already considered and rejected, or models that conflict with values.

**SLT 4.3: "I can design a project that generates value without external funding."**
- **Partial dimensions**: Assessment — same as 4.2. General design thinking is strong but Gimbalabs-specific feasibility needs input.
- **What I would need**: Examples of past projects that were close to self-sustaining, or community assets that could be leveraged (Andamio platform, educational content library, network).
- **Risk if coached without context**: Lesson is generic rather than grounded in what Gimbalabs specifically has to work with.

## Set-Level Observations

- **Strength cluster**: General analytical frameworks, sustainability models, project design, documentation strategy. I'm strong on the "how to think about it" — the scaffolding and framing questions.
- **Weakness cluster**: Lived community experience. Almost every gap is the same shape: I have the written record but not the institutional memory, political context, or current team dynamics.
- **Critical path**: The single highest-leverage resource is a **community conversation about Modules 1-3** — what patterns matter, why projects ended, what the primary driver means in practice. This unlocks 7 SLTs.
- **Sequencing implication**: Module 6.1 (How To Guide) can be built immediately. Everything else benefits from the community doing Modules 1-3 first as a collective exercise, which then feeds into Modules 4-7. The course is designed to be done together — it shouldn't be pre-built and delivered, it should be facilitated.
- **Confabulation risk zones**: SLT 3.2 (why projects ended) and SLT 5.1 (current ecosystem problems). These are areas where I could generate plausible-sounding but wrong answers. Better to frame these as community exercises than expert-delivered content.
- **Key insight**: This course is less "content to deliver" and more "facilitation framework to run." The Exploration lessons should be structured as guided conversations, not lectures. The community IS the content.
