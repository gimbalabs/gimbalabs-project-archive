# Cardano Community Feedback Tooling

## Gimbalabs Primary Driver

To provide open, replicable safe spaces to learn, to explore, and to empower individuals and organizations anywhere so that we can solve meaningful problems

## Proposal Driver

**Current Situation:**
The Cardano ecosystem lacks consistent, structured tooling for gathering and acting on community feedback. Governance processes generate opinions but feedback rarely flows back into the tools and protocols people actually use. Builders ship features without structured input from users. Community members express frustration in forums and social media, but that signal is diffuse and hard to act on.

**Effects:**
Builders make assumptions about what the community needs. Governance decisions lack grounded community input. Feedback that does exist is scattered across Discord, Twitter, forum posts, and town halls with no aggregation or synthesis. The gap between "what people say they want" and "what gets built" persists.

**Need:**
Gimbalabs needs to facilitate the creation of open-source feedback tooling and a recurring community practice around using it — so that community input is structured, visible, and actionable by builders and governance participants.

---

## Proposal

### 1. Title or Brief Description

Run weekly meetings to design, build, and iterate on open-source feedback tooling for the Cardano community.

### 2. Background or Rationale

Gimbalabs has a track record of running open weekly sessions (Live Coding, Playground, Dev Standups) that produce real output. This proposal applies the same format to a specific problem: feedback tooling. The goal is not to build one app — it's to establish a practice of building feedback infrastructure in the open, with community participation.

This connects to broader Cardano governance maturity. As on-chain governance scales, the ecosystem needs better mechanisms for structured feedback beyond poll-style voting.

This work is already in flight. [midnight-pbl.io](https://midnight-pbl.io) and [cardano-xp.io](https://cardano-xp.io) are live applications where community feedback tooling is being developed and tested. This proposal formalizes and funds the weekly coordination that's already happening around these projects.

### 3. Intended Outcome

- A weekly meeting cadence (1 hour) focused on feedback tooling design and development
- Open-source tooling that any Cardano project can use to collect, structure, and surface community feedback
- Documentation of feedback patterns and tooling decisions, published openly
- A small but committed group of contributors who show up, build, and iterate

### 4. Details of the Proposal

**Weekly meetings — Thursdays at Open Spaces time (1 hour):**
- First 15 min: review what was built since last week, demo working software
- Next 30 min: build together — pair programming, design discussions, or user testing
- Final 15 min: decide what to work on next week, assign owners

**Tooling scope (starting points, not commitments):**
- Structured feedback collection (beyond free-text — tagged, categorized, attributable)
- Feedback aggregation and synthesis views
- Integration points with existing Cardano governance tools
- API-first design so other projects can plug in

**Open by default:**
- Meetings recorded and published
- Code in a public repo
- Decisions documented in the repo

### 5. Impact and Benefits

- Builders get structured community input they can act on
- Community members see their feedback go somewhere concrete
- Gimbalabs demonstrates its role as infrastructure for community coordination
- Reusable tooling that outlasts the meeting series

### 6. Evaluation Criteria

- Meetings happening consistently (weekly for at least 12 weeks)
- At least one deployed tool or prototype by week 8
- 3+ regular contributors beyond the organizer
- At least one external Cardano project adopts or integrates the tooling

### 7. Timeline

| Milestone | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| First meeting | Within 2 weeks of approval |
| Initial prototype | Week 4 |
| First external feedback | Week 6 |
| Deployable v1 | Week 8 |
| Review and decide on continuation | Week 12 |

### 8. Resources Required

- 1 organizer committing 3-4 hours/week (meeting + prep + follow-up)
- Public GitHub repo
- Video conferencing (existing Gimbalabs Zoom/Discord)
- **15,000 ADA** budget allocated as follows:

| Category | Allocation | Purpose |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| Hosting | 1,250 ADA | Infrastructure for deployed tooling (servers, domains, CI/CD) |
| Sponsored transactions & on-chain fees | 2,000 ADA | Cover transaction fees so users can interact without holding ADA |
| Project admin | 2,000 ADA | Organizing meetings, documentation, reporting |
| Project team | 9,750 ADA | Contributors building and iterating on the tooling |

### 9. Responsibilities

- **Organizer (James Dunseith):** Schedule meetings, set agenda, maintain repo, ensure documentation
- **Contributors:** Show up, build, review PRs, test tooling
- **Gimbalabs:** Provide platform (streaming, repo hosting, community channels)

### 10. Potential Risks and Mitigation Strategies

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Low attendance | Keep meetings short and output-focused. People come back when they see working software. |
| Scope creep | Each week has one focus. The repo README defines what's in scope. |
| Tooling doesn't get adopted | Build with real use cases from day one. Dogfood with Gimbalabs governance first. |
| Organizer burnout | Keep the commitment lightweight. If energy drops, pause rather than push through. |

### 11. Review and Adaptation Plan

- **Week 4:** Check-in — is the format working? Adjust meeting structure if needed.
- **Week 8:** Mid-point review — is the tooling useful? Pivot or continue based on what we've learned.
- **Week 12:** Full review — decide whether to continue, expand, or close. Document what worked.
