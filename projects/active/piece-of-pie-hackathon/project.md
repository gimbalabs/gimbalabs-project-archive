# Piece Of Pie Hackathon by Gimbalabs

## Gimbalabs Primary Driver

To provide open, replicable safe spaces to learn, to explore, and to empower individuals and organizations anywhere so that we can solve meaningful problems.

---

## Proposal Driver

**Current Situation:**
No lightweight structure exists to encourage consistent building, sharing, and real-world experimentation without heavy coordination or competition.

**Effects:**

- Isolated building
- Low visibility of progress
- Limited push toward real users

**Need:**
A simple, low-friction system that rewards:

- Consistent participation
- Public progress
- Real-world outcomes

---

## Proposal

### 1. Description

A 12-week participation-driven hackathon where contributors earn equal shares of fixed reward pools by meeting clear criteria.

---

### 2. Format

- Duration: 12 weeks
- First 2 weeks = **Enrollment Period**
- Enrollment closes after Week 2 (no new entries)
- One participant → one project only (else disqualified)

---

### 3. Reward Structure
The hackathon uses a reward structure divided into separate pools, referred to as "Pies".  

- 10,000 ADA → Builders Pie (equal split)
- 2,000 ADA → Real User Pie (equal split)
- 1,000 ADA → Feedback Pie (credit-weighted)
- 3,000 ADA → Admins (fixed)

A total of 10,000 ADA is allocated to the Builders Pie, which is distributed as an equal split among qualifying participants.

An additional 2,000 ADA is allocated to the Real User Pie, also distributed equally among participants who meet the criteria.

The Feedback Pie consists of 1,000 ADA and is distributed based on credit-weighted contributions.

Finally, 3,000 ADA is allocated as a fixed amount for administrative support.

---

### 4. Builder Requirements

- Start with empty repo at hackathon start
- Public repo required (until final payout)
- Visible commit history
- Minimum 1 tweet per week (no misses)
- Tweet must include:
  #gimbalabs #pieceofpie #hackathon @gimbalabs
- Final presentation must include:
  - Live demo
  - Public repo
  - All weekly tweet links

Missing any week → disqualified

---

### 5. Real User Requirements

- Must qualify as builder
- At least 1 paying user
- Must present:
  - Payment proof (receipt / tx)
  - Customer acquisition story
- Must be shared publicly

---

### 6. Feedback System

- Live interaction (Zoom/Meet/etc.)
- Must be recorded
- 1 interaction = 1 credit
- Max 15 credits per person
- 1,000 ADA split proportionally

example: If Alice does the max 15 feedback sessions, and Bill does 5 and Charles does 10, that's 30 total points.  1000 / 30 is 33.30.  So, Alice get 15 times 33.3 (500). Bill is 5 times 33.30 (166).  And, Charles is 10 times 33.3 (330)



---

### 7. Administration

Admins are responsible for:

- Coordination
- Participant tracking
- Hackathon website + enrollment flow
- Final presentation Meeting and logistics

Verification handled via public presentations

---

### 8. Key Principle

Qualify through consistent, verifiable participation — not competition

---

### 9. Timeline

- Week 0: Announcement
- Weeks 1–2: Enrollment Period
- Weeks 1–12: Build period
- End Week 12: Final presentations
- Post: Payout distribution

---

## 10. Risks

| Risk           | Mitigation                |
| -------------- | ------------------------- |
| Drop-offs      | Strict weekly requirement |
| Fake users     | Public proof + story      |
| Low quality    | Qualification rules       |
| Feedback abuse | Recorded interactions     |

---

## 11. Outcomes

- Working products
- Public build logs
- Real users (even minimal)
- Stronger builder culture
