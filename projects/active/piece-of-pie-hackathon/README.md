## Rules

1
Software projects only
Web2, Web3, or no-code is fine. The project must still be software.

2
Teams can have one or more participants
Solo entries and team entries are both allowed.

3
All participant names are mandatory
Every person on the project must be listed during registration. No unnamed teammates.

4
One participant can only join one project
If a participant is involved in multiple projects, the project is disqualified. The idea is to work on a single project to ensure focus and quality.

5
Each project needs one official public repo
The submitted repo link becomes the official source of truth for that project.

6
The repo must start empty
It must be public at registration and remain public until final payout.

7
Weekly public progress is required
At least one public tweet every week. No misses.

8
Weekly tweets must be easy to verify
They should clearly show progress and include the required hackathon tags.

9
Feedback evidence must come from recorded interaction
Google Meet, Zoom, or another recorded interaction is acceptable.

10
Verification happens through public evidence
Repo history, public posts, recorded feedback, and final presentation are the basis for qualification.

## Register

Registration should happen through a Google Form, not a custom on-site form.

What the Google Form must require
Project title
Primary contact name
All participant names
Contact email
Short project summary
Official public Git repository link
Agreement to weekly tweet requirement
Agreement to one-project-per-participant rule
