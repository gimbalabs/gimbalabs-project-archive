# Sponsor Gimbalabs contributors to Cardano African Summit: Nairobi

Links:
- [Cardano Africa Tech Summit 2026](https://luma.com/cats26)

## Gimbalabs Primary Driver
To provide open, replicable safe spaces to learn, to explore, and to empower individuals and organizations anywhere so that we can solve meaningful problems

## Proposal

### 1. Title or Brief Description
**Cardano Kenya February Trip**
A short, in-person collaboration trip for four Gimbalabs contributors traveling from Nigeria to Kenya, centered around a Cardano event, shared accommodation, and structured collaboration time.

### 2. Background or Rationale
While Gimbalabs contributors collaborate effectively online, sustained trust, shared context, and long-term coordination especially across African regions are difficult to fully achieve remotely.

Kenya hosts one of the most active Cardano ecosystems and community builders in Africa example Lido nation, Mercy, wada communities and many others, the Cardano Africa Summit (CAT) presents a rare opportunity for concentrated learning, relationship-building, and ecosystem alignment.

This trip is designed as a focused contributor residency: a lightweight, cost-conscious model that combines event participation with intentional collaboration and documentation. The goal is to strengthen African contributor networks and accelerate follow-up initiatives after the event.

### 3. Intended Outcome
- Strengthened relationships among Cardano/Gimbalabs contributors
- Shared learning from the Kenya Cardano event(s)
- Clear documentation of insights, connections, and follow-up actions
- Increased coordination across African Cardano builders post-trip

### 4. Details of the Proposal
- **Participants:** 3 - 4 contributors traveling from Nigeria
  - Emmanuel
  - Jethro
  - one more additional - from blockPrint
  - Lewis in Kenya
- **Location:** Kenya (city aligned with the Cardano event location)
- **Accommodation:** Shared Airbnb for the duration of the trip
- **Activities include:**
  - Attendance at Cardano Africa Summit(CAT) three days event
    - Feb 11th - 13th
  - Daily informal sync sessions (reflection, planning, debriefs)
  - Optional co-working sessions
  - Community engagement with local builders where possible
- **Documentation:** Short daily notes + a final trip summary shared with Gimbalabs

### 5. Impact and Benefits
- Builds trust and shared context that is difficult to achieve remotely
- Encourages idea sharing between regional communities
- Produces reusable insights for future Gimbalabs or Cardano collaborations especially african builders
- Strengthens credibility and trust for Gimbalabs contributors who are also entrepreneurs, for example:
  - Blockprint building trust with the wider Cardano community
  - Tracom Academy and related initiatives led by Lewis
- Demonstrates a replicable model for small, focused contributor residencies

### 6. Evaluation Criteria
- All participants attend summit
- Social-media post and feedbacks
- At least one shared written summary or report produced
- Clear follow-up actions or collaborations identified post-trip
- Positive participant feedback on value of in-person coordination

### 7. Timeline
- **January:** Planning, budgeting, accommodation booking
- **February:**
  - 11th - 13th
  - Travel, event participation, collaboration sessions
- **Post-Trip (1-2 weeks):** Documentation, review, and sharing outcomes
- Covers travel, accommodation, local transport, and basic coordination costs

### 8. Resources Required
Overall budgets estimate - 13,500 ADA @ 0.36 = $4900
- Flight and transport expenses to and fro - $1200 x 3 = 3600 + Lewis $300 = $3900
- Accommodation AirBnb 4 days - $500
- Foods and beverages - $500

### 9. Responsibilities
- Trip Coordination & Reporting: Emmanuel & Lewis
- Event Engagement & Community Notes: All participants
- Local Coordination (Kenya): Lewis
- Post-Trip Documentation & Summary: Shared responsibility, with a designated editor

### 10. Potential Risks and Mitigation Strategies
- **Travel or visa delays:** Early planning and contingency timelines
- **Budget overruns:** other unforseen expenses may arise
- **Low post-trip follow-through:** Defined documentation deliverables and follow-up actions before departure
- **Scheduling conflicts during the event:** Flexible daily sync sessions rather than rigid schedules

### 11. Review and Adaptation Plan
- Conduct a short retrospective within two weeks of the trip
- Review outcomes against evaluation criteria
- Identify what worked and what should be improved
- Use findings to refine a repeatable model for future Gimbalabs contributor trips or residencies
