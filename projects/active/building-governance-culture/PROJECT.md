# Building a Governance Culture at Gimbalabs (testing phase)

## Gimbalabs Primary Driver
To provide open, replicable safe spaces to learn, to explore, and to empower individuals and organizations anywhere so that we can solve meaningful problems

## Proposal Driver

**Current Situation:**
Low participation in Cardano governance by the Gimbalabs community.

Action in governance leads to burnout - never having enough time - "people can't imagine another governance culture"

**Effects of that:**
Gimbalabs will forever be a neutral observer when it comes to Cardano governance.

**Core Assumption:**
Governance starts as a practice and eventually becomes part of the culture.

---

## Proposal

### 1. Title or Brief Description
Add a structured 10–15 minute segment to each Open Spaces meeting dedicated to one active "to-be-voted" proposal. The host provides a concise summary (problem, scope, budget, impact), followed by focused discussion for clarifications, risks, concerns, and relevance before voting.

This will be a test run that will span through 8 Gimbalabs Open Governance Spaces.

### 2. Intended Outcome
- Cardano Governance becomes a part of Gimbalabs culture
- Gimbalabs community gets to practice hosting in small sandboxed time slots
- Encourages the community to participate in voting through the Gimbalabs dRep portal

### 3. Impact and Benefits
- Gimbalabs becomes an active voice in Cardano Governance
- Gimbalabs dRep can be automated and community driven
- Gimbalabs becomes a respected voice in governance circles

### 4. Evaluation Criteria
- Each week we have one or more community members coming forward to host the 15 min governance slot
- More votes on dRep community platform

### 5. Timeline
8 Gimbalabs Governance Spaces from the time the proposal is approved.

### 6. Resources Required
There will be a small 'thank you' compensation of 25 ADA for the governance hosts each week.
For the test run of 8 weeks the budget will be 200 ADA.

### 7. Responsibilities
Newman and Harsha will run two hand holding sessions each and the last four sessions will be completely voluntary and open for the community.

### 8. Potential Risks and Mitigation Strategies
The testing phase is reasonably short and low risk with a budget of 200 ADA.
