## Proposal

### 1. Title or Brief Description

Making Gimbalabs a DRep (Delegated Representative) Collective

### 2. Background or Rationale

After four years of establishing a standing in the Cardano ecosystem, Gimbalabs has gained recognition for community-driven education and contributing to blockchain innovation. As a trusted and respected community within Catalyst and the broader ecosystem, there is a shared sentiment that the Gimbalabs community should take a more active role in governance as a DRep. This proposal seeks to streamline the process for collective decision-making by **building** + **practicing using** validators to support shared DRep structures.

### 3. Intended Outcome

The intended outcome is for Gimbalabs to:

1. Develop on-chain validators and off-chain APIs to:
   1. Help people make proposals
   2. Allow people to make collective decisions
2. Test and document the use of these tools in our collaborative spaces, so that we can create a model that can be taught and adopted
3. Using an on-chain system for voting and validators for group decision-making
4. At the end of this project Gimbalabs would be a functioning DRep that:
   1. is collectively managed with these tools
   2. provides access to Educational materials and documentation of the DREP process
   3. allowing the community to participate collectively in governance decisions

### 4. Details of the Proposal

- **Voting Mechanism**: A single NFT will represent the DRep identity. This NFT will be placed in a validator or multi-sig account where the logic for community voting takes place.
- **Initial Setup**: The voting mechanism could start with a majority-wins rule to allow for quick adoption. Over time, feedback from the community will drive the evolution of the decision-making process.
- **Validator Use**: Using a validator to handle decision processes simplifies who can vote, based on token ownership. This avoids off-chain determinations for multi-sig setups.
- **Long-Term Flexibility**: A smart contract that checks for token ownership in the voting process ensures dynamic management of the DRep, allowing for future adjustments without changing the DRep identity.

### 5. Impact and Benefits

- **Simplified Voting**: Utilizing validators to streamline decision-making reduces friction in the voting process.
- **Community Empowerment**: Members who possess contributor tokens can directly participate in governance.
- **Scalability**: The proposal allows for future expansion of voting mechanisms and governance structures.

### 6. Evaluation Criteria

- Feedback from the community on the usability and fairness of the voting mechanism.

### 7. Timeline

- **Phase 1**: Setup initial voting system with majority-wins using a validator or multi-sig account.
- **Phase 2**: Gather feedback from the community and refine the voting process.
- **Phase 3**: Expand to more advanced smart contracts

### 8. Resources Required

- Developer resources to implement validator-based voting and smart contract logic.
- Multi-sig or validator platform, potentially using tools like Mesh for the initial setup.
- Time from community members to participate in governance discussions and voting.

### 9. Responsibilities

- **Gimbalabs Team**: Overall implementation and guidance on governance structure.
- **DRep Lead**: Managing and documenting the actual DRep registration of Gimbalabs.
- **Developers**: Technical setup of validators, multi-sig accounts, and smart contracts.
- **Community Members**: Participation in voting and providing feedback on the process.

### 10. Potential Risks and Mitigation Strategies

- **Risk**: Community disengagement due to complex voting mechanisms.
  - **Mitigation**: Start with a simple majority-wins rule to get the process going quickly and adjust based on feedback.
- **Risk**: Token ownership misuse, such as transferring the token to non-community members.
  - **Mitigation**: Utilize multi-sig wallets or smart contracts to enforce community decisions on token transfers.

### 11. Review and Adaptation Plan

- Regular feedback sessions with the community to assess the effectiveness of the voting process.
- Incremental adjustments based on real-world use and challenges encountered during voting.
