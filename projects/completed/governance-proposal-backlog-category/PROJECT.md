# Governance Proposal: introduce category "backlog"

## Gimbalabs Primary Driver
To provide open, replicable safe spaces to learn, to explore, and to empower individuals and organizations anywhere so that we can solve meaningful problems

## Proposal Driver
Currently, proposals can stagnate in the "in-progress" category without any actual progress being made. This can lead to a backlog of proposals and inefficient use of resources. Create a system that automatically updates the status of proposals from "in-progress" to "backlog" after a predetermined time interval.

## Proposal

### Title
Automate Proposal Status Updates

### Background or Rationale
Currently, proposals can stagnate in the "in-progress" category without any actual progress being made. This can lead to a backlog of proposals and inefficient use of resources.

### Intended Outcome
To create a system that automatically updates the status of proposals from "in-progress" to "backlog" after a predetermined time interval, ensuring that proposals are regularly reviewed and updated.

### Details of the Proposal
- Set a time interval (e.g., 30 days) after which a proposal will be automatically moved from "in-progress" to "backlog" if no progress has been made.
- Develop a notification system to alert proposal owners and stakeholders when a proposal is approaching or has reached the time interval.
- Provide an option for proposal owners to manually update the status of their proposal or provide justification for why the proposal should remain in the "in-progress" category.

### Impact and Benefits
- Encourages regular review and updates of proposals
- Prevents stagnation of proposals and inefficient use of resources
- Enhances transparency and accountability in the proposal process

### Evaluation Criteria
- Effectiveness of the automated system in updating proposal statuses
- Reduction in the number of stagnant proposals
- User satisfaction with the notification system and manual override option

### Timeline
- Development of automated system: 2 weeks
- Testing and refinement: 1 week
- Implementation and rollout: 1 week

### Resources Required
- Development team: 1 developer, 1 QA engineer
- Project management: 1 project manager

### Responsibilities
- Development team: Develop and test the automated system
- Project manager: Oversee development, testing, and implementation
- Proposal owners: Regularly review and update proposals, respond to notifications

### Potential Risks and Mitigation Strategies
- Risk: Automated system errors or glitches
- Mitigation: Regular testing and monitoring, escalation procedures for errors

### Review and Adaptation Plan
- Regular review of the automated system's effectiveness (every 3 months)
- Gather feedback from proposal owners and stakeholders
- Refine the automated system and notification process based on feedback and evaluation criteria
