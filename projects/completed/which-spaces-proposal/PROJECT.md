# "Which Spaces" proposal

## Gimbalabs Primary Driver
To provide open, replicable safe spaces to **learn, explore, and empower individuals and organizations** anywhere so that we can **solve meaningful problems**

## Proposal Driver
To consent on Spaces that the Gimbalabs community will host, compensation for hosts, space marketing, and other administrative needs.

## Proposal

### 1. Title or Brief Description
Based on the 'What Spaces' workshop, the spaces below are being proposed to be hosted by the Gimbalabs community:
- **Live coding Sessions (Learning): Wednesdays at 14:30 UTC**
- **Gimbalabs Open Spaces (Governing): Thursdays at 14:30 UTC**

**Additional Spaces:**
There are some fantastic space ideas that were proposed and the expectation is that anyone interested in hosting new spaces will put forward a proposal to host the space.

**Space Needs:**
- **Marketing:** Awareness building leading up to the space (separate marketing proposal)
- **Hosting**
- **Administrative:** Notetaker, Recording the space, Edit recording (optional), Uploading Recordings to the marketing/Catalyst Milestones repo, Google Drive (for storage)

### 2. Background or Rationale
The rationale behind running the current spaces is a mix of flagship spaces and community interest/involvement as per the What Spaces workshop.
- 'Gimbalabs Live Coding' is a space that supports PBLs and learning in general.
- 'Gimbalabs open spaces' is a mandatory space for us to make decisions on the direction of Gimbalabs and treasury spending.

**List of spaces as per value added:**
- **Gimbalabs Open Spaces** - 6 stickies
- **Gimbalabs Playground** - 5 stickies
- **PPBL Live Coding** - 4 stickies
- **Cardano Go Live Coding** - 3 stickies
- **Mesh Live Coding** - 3 stickies

*Note: The number of stickies in the value section is used as a metric because it reflects the level of engagement and significance that members attribute to a space. A higher number of stickies suggests that either many members find the space valuable or that a few members feel particularly strongly about it.*

**Host Compensation:** 200 ADA/session

**Poll Results:**
Please vote on your preferred compensation for space hosts:
- 0 - 100 ADA/session
- 100 - 200 ADA/session (selected)
- 200 - 300 ADA/session
- > 300 ADA/session

**Marketing Team Compensation:** 50 ADA/space each week
**Marketing Plan Creation:** 300 ADA
**Additional Budget for Tools:** 1500 ADA (can be unlocked through proposals)
**Milestones and Administrative Work Compensation:** 2% of total budget

### 4. Impact and Benefits

**Educational Benefits:**
- **Starting Point in Cardano Technology**: Gave a foundation to understand and engage with the development ecosystem.
- **Teaching Opportunities**: Can be used to teach Cardano development in schools (PPBL and Mesh PBL).
- **Programming Skills Development**: Gained confidence in learning programming, with opportunities to learn and build.

**Community and Networking:**
- **Meeting New People**: Helped meet new people on similar journeys and make friends.
- **Collaborative Environment**: Provides a virtual office-like space and a truly open collaborative area where big conversations can happen and ideas take root.
- **Belonging and Empowerment**: Offers a feeling of belonging to a larger group, empowering members within the community.

**Governance and Management Practices:**
- **Governance Experimentation**: Explored non-hierarchical governance and is the beginning of Gimbalabs' Governance experiment, aligning with Cardano's implementation.
- **S3 Practices**: Opportunities to practice and learn S3 management and governance practices, enhancing understanding and application.

**Problem Solving and Support:**
- **Support When Stuck**: Available help when encountering difficulties with modules.
- **Q&A and Collaboration**: Benefited from live Q&A sessions that facilitate collaboration and problem-solving.

**Ideation and Project Development:**
- **Idea Presentation and Leadership**: Offered chances to present ideas, lead conversations, and develop new projects.
- **Source and Topics Exploration**: Introduced to various topics and themes, encouraging thinking about the source of information and ideas.

**Technological Collaboration:**
- **Mesh Features and Collaboration**: Learned about Mesh features, fostering building collaborations, and creating useful apps.
- **Partnership with Blink Labs**: Engaged in collaborative efforts with external entities like Blink Labs.

### 5. Intended Outcome
The intended outcome of the proposal:
1. Space hosts are assigned for each month
2. Marketing team is assigned
3. Marketing plan is created (a separate budget is assigned in the finance section)
4. Zoom credentials and repo permissions are shared with space hosts and marketing team
5. **Artifacts for each space:** Space recording, Saving recording in the correct folder, Saving notes in the folder (optional)
6. **Marketing Spaces:** The minimum requirement is 2 posts before each session on 2 social media platforms.
7. **Marketing Artifacts:** Saving links to each post in the correct space folder
8. Milestones for each month are submitted and approved
9. Tensions backlog is created and reviewed on the first open space for each month

### 6. Evaluation Criteria
Evaluating through the tensions backlog which is reviewed each month.

### 10. Potential Risks and Mitigation Strategies
If a specific host is not able to make a specific session, it's suggested to find a replacement or make an announcement 3 days in advance. This gives time to decide what's to be done if there is no replacement host.

### 11. Review and Adaptation Plan
Create a tension backlog (refer to S3) where anyone involved in the spaces (hosts, marketing team, attendees, administrative team, etc.) can record their tensions. These backlogs will be visited each month during the Open Spaces to discuss and decide on improvements to the spaces or the overall proposal.
