# Initial Notes: Gimbalabs DRep vote on Constitution

## Gimbalabs Primary Driver
To provide open, replicable safe spaces to learn, to explore, and to empower individuals and organizations anywhere so that we can solve meaningful problems

## Vote Options Analysis

### Gimbalabs should vote yes...
- Forces us to have 'skin in the game' - focuses the conversation - without a constitution we stay in the liminal space of vague discussion
- The ambiguity of the constitution - whilst not ideal - can provide scope for positive change, not just potential negatives
- The real challenges lie in future voting proposals. More importantly the details of budget proposals and how treasury funds are dispersed. Here I believe the full weight of our process and energy should be placed
- A rationale for a yes Vote that echos the discussion - [Google Drive document](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1QkQQp9qYT5BAQJ627QfNEkV-PkEQYYNS/view)

### Gimbalabs should vote no...
- Scott's rationale for a no vote: [GitHub PDF](https://github.com/st8tikratio/cardano_DRep/blob/main/votes/january2025/replace-interim-constitution-governance-action-justification.pdf)
- Q has a rationale for a no vote: coming soon
- The governance action does not provide optimal protection for GL, its members and delegates from activities of bad actors.
- The manner in which the governance item was debated or created is considered unethical (according to who?)

### Gimbalabs should abstain...
We choose to abstain because:

1. **Supports the Process, But Not Fully Aligned** - A voter may respect the legitimacy of the vote but feel that the proposal is not quite right. Rather than voting yes or no on something they only partially support, abstaining allows them to acknowledge the process without committing to a stance they don't fully endorse.

2. **Maintains Neutrality in a Divided Debate** - In situations where a proposal is highly polarized, some voters may prefer to stay neutral rather than align with one side or the other. Abstaining ensures they don't contribute to escalating division while still participating in the governance process.

3. **Recognizes Uncertainty or Lack of Clarity** - If a voter feels they don't have enough information or the proposal is too complex to confidently decide, abstaining is a responsible action. Rather than making an uninformed choice, they signal that they trust the community to decide while stepping back from the decision.

### Gimbalabs should not be a DRep...
- There is a conflict of interest as GL is too closely aligned to Intersect and other close organizations. Voting in either direction could be considered favoritism to a partner organization or could be detrimental to ongoing GL projects. This would reduce Gimbalabs voting against their own interest in order to garner favor with partner organizations

### Gimbalabs should vote no confidence...
- The group generally believes the current governance processes are antithetical to the GL mission or its members.
